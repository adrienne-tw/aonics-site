How it works

  • Start in plain language

    Photon meets operators where they already work—ask for a scan, a route, or context on a block, and keep the conversation tied to the live plant.

  • Dispatch to the field

    A mission request becomes real work on the ground—status and progress stay visible so dispatch and crews share the same picture of what is running.

  • Structured findings

    Results roll up into rows you can sort, filter, and hand off—component, location, defect type, and string context in one place instead of scattered notes.

  • Map and assistant together

    The live layout, filters, and assistant sit side by side—highlight a block, deploy a rover, and read back totals without jumping between tools.

  • Evidence down to the component

    Open the capture that backs a finding—thermal cues, labels, and readings—so the control room can prioritize and the crew knows exactly where to look.

Frequently asked questions

What is Photon in the Aonics platform?

Photon is the conversational layer that sits on top of your site context—maps, missions, and findings—so operators can ask plain-language questions, dispatch work, and review detections without jumping between disconnected tools.

Does it work with how we already tag blocks and strings?

The platform is designed around utility-scale hierarchies—sites, blocks, strings, and components—so your existing naming and GIS context can carry through. We focus on aligning language in the product with what crews already use in the field.

Can we start with a pilot before rolling out fleet-wide?

Yes. Teams often begin with a bounded scope—one site or a subset of blocks—to validate workflows, train dispatchers, and tune reporting before expanding missions and integrations across the portfolio.

How is mission and inspection data handled securely?

Data is treated as operational evidence: encrypted in transit, access-controlled by role, and retained with an audit-friendly trail so both the control room and the field share a single source of truth for what was observed and when.

Who uses Photon day to day?

Dispatchers and performance engineers use it for planning and triage; technicians use it to confirm routes, close out work, and attach context from the plant. Everyone works off the same structured findings instead of ad hoc screenshots and side threads.
